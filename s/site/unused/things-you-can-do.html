Things You Can Do

We need your help to make LIFE possible for the more than 120,000 people waiting for a second chance at life.

Register to be a Donor

Commit now to giving life to others when you die. Each registration serves as a symbol of hope to every patient awaiting a lifesaving or healing transplant.

Consider Being a Living Donor

Living donation is an opportunity to save a life while you are still living. It is not covered by your donor registration. 

Financially Support Our Work

Contribute Online

Every dollar that is contributed by the public to Donate Life America goes directly towards increasing the number of organ, eye and tissue donors, one person at a time. Whether your contribution is made in honor of a loved one currently waiting for a transplant, in honor of someone who lost his or her life in the battle against time, or just because you recognize the urgency, it will save lives in the future.

Mail in Contributions

Fundraise for Life

Creating a fundraising page in honor of a life event, in memory of a loved one or just because you know the need is great, is an incredible way to support Donate Life America and our mission. 

Monthly Giving

Join our Donate LifeLine to provide the steady, reliable support we so urgently need to save and heal more lives through donation. At the time of your donation, just indicate you would like to make it a monthly contribution. It is that easy to make an everlasting impact on our mission!

To learn more about how you can financially support the work of Donate Life America, please visit Give to DLA.

Educate Others

Use these resources to educate others about the importance of organ, eye and tissue donation.

Protect Your Health

The best long-term solution to solving the organ donor crisis is to avoid the need for transplantation.

  • Have your blood pressure checked routinely.
  • Exercise regularly.
  • Limit foods high in salt, cholesterol and saturated fats, such as fried foods that can clog the arteries.
  • Visit your doctor at least once a year and get tested for diabetes and other illnesses that can lead to organ failure.

Be socially responsible
